Sinukwan Festival

First up isone of the many festivals held in the wondeful city of San Fernando. It is no other than
Sinukwan Festival! Visitors all over the country and the world are describing this festival as the
"Essence of Kapampangan Spirit".

The Sinukwan Festival is a culture and week-long festival mainly focused on giving praise to the Kapampangan
god Aring Sinukwan, who is believed to be a deity since ancient times. Several competitions highlight this
grand festival such as street dances, photography competitions, pageants, and even local cooking showdown!
Moreover, countless food fairs are scattered around the city giving the visitors a chance to taste the ever-famous
culinary brilliance of the Kapampangan people.

Sinukwan Festival is held every December, coinciding with several regular holidays of the year. That being
said, the local community expects thousands of visitors to flock to San Fernando City. So, if you’re planning
to experience the wonderful celebration of the Sinukwan Festival, you’d better plan ahead!

Philippine International Hot air baloon festival

Ah, of course, this list wouldn't be complete without the biggest and longest-running aviation sports in the country!
Yup, it's the Hot Air Balloon Festival of Clark, Pampanga. It is a four-day event featuring hundreds of multicolored
and designed hot air balloons with pilots from all over the world in participation!

Did you know that the Hot Air Balloon Festival was initiated to boost the economy of Pampanga after the devastating
eruption of Mount Pinatubo? Right. It was the idea of then Tourism Secretary Mina Gabor. Well, look at Pampanga now,
I'd say that the province was able to overcome these challenges and prospered beautifully.

The Hot Air Balloon Festival itself is aesthetically pleasing, but the festivities also feature other activities such
as freestyle aerobatics, flag jumps, kitemaking, remote-control airplane and helicopter competition, and many
more! Unleash the daredevil in you as you try all these breathtaking activities!

Giant Lantern Festival (ligligan parul)

Yet another grand festivity is located at none other than the city of San Fernando! The Giant Lantern Festival or Ligligan
Parul is one of the most anticipated events in Pampanga every year mainly because of its aesthetically satisfying giant parol
lanterns! Because of this festival, the city of San Fernando was given the nickname "Christmas Capital of the Philippines".

You will truly feel the Christmas spirit living within the hearts of Filipinos during the Giant Lantern Festival.
The festivities were intended to coincide with the Simbang Gabi, as lantern competitions get bigger and brighter as
Christmas Day comes closer and closer. The most important activity of the Ligligan Parul is the Lubenas, it is a nine
(9)-day novena and evening procession on the eve of every Simbang Gabi.

Aside from giant lantern competitions and activities, visitors also won't be disappointed with the wide variety of food
selections available in the city! If I were you, I'd take this perfect chance to reward myself with a gastronomic feast
of Kapampangan food!
